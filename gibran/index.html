Kahlil Gibran: A 12-Week Guided Reading Journey

San Francisco Reading Group

A contemplative exploration of Kahlil Gibran's major works through weekly readings, in-person discussions, and shared reflection. This journey is designed for personal enrichment and deepened understanding, not academic mastery.

Join Our Reading Group

We're forming an intimate reading community in San Francisco to journey through Gibran's transformative works together. Over twelve weeks, we'll read chronologically through his major writings—from the rebellious early stories to the timeless wisdom of The Prophet and the polyphonic beauty of Jesus, The Son of Man.

This is not a lecture series or academic seminar. It's a space for slow reading, honest reflection, and genuine conversation about justice, love, exile, spirituality, and what it means to be human.

  • Format: Weekly in-person meetings in San Francisco + asynchronous group chat
  • Duration: 12 weeks, following the curriculum below
  • Meeting Day/Time: To be determined based on group availability
  • Location: San Francisco (specific location TBD based on group consensus)
  • Group Chat: Platform to be decided by the group for weekly reflections and discussion
  • Reading Load: Approximately 40-60 pages per week
  • Commitment: Show up with curiosity, read slowly, engage authentically

The readings are all available for free online or through affordable print editions. We'll read slowly, reread freely, and allow space for silence and dialogue. The discussion questions are starting points, not prescriptions.

12-Week Curriculum

Reading List Overview

Weeks 1–2 Spirits Rebellious (selections)
Week 3 The Broken Wings
Week 4 The Madman
Week 5 The Forerunner
Week 6 Sand and Foam
Weeks 7–8 The Prophet
Weeks 9–10 Jesus, The Son of Man
Week 11 The Garden of the Prophet
Week 12 Beloved Prophet (letters) + Review

Plus biographical chapters from Robin Waterfield's Prophet throughout the journey.

Week 1 — Roots: Lebanon, Loss, and the Birth of a Voice

Primary Reading:
  • Spirits Rebellious: "The Cry of the Graves"
Supplementary Reading:
  • Robin Waterfield, Prophet, Chapters 1–2
Discussion:
  • How does injustice function as a spiritual catalyst?
  • What early signs of the prophetic voice appear?
  • Where do compassion and anger intersect?

Week 2 — Exile, Immigration, and Rebellion

Primary Reading:
  • Spirits Rebellious: "Khalil the Heretic"
Supplementary Reading:
  • Robin Waterfield, Prophet, Chapters 3–4
Discussion:
  • When is rebellion morally necessary?
  • How does exile sharpen identity?
  • What authority structures are being challenged?

Week 3 — Love and Constraint

Primary Reading:
  • The Broken Wings (entire book)
Supplementary Reading:
  • Beloved Prophet: Letters dated 1910–1913 (pp. 39–82)
Discussion:
  • Is this a love story or social critique?
  • How does love awaken suffering?
  • What is sacrificed in the name of tradition?

Week 4 — The Mask of the Madman

Primary Reading:
  • The Madman (entire book)
Supplementary Reading:
  • Robin Waterfield, Prophet, Chapter 6
Discussion:
  • Why does truth wear masks?
  • Which parable lingered longest?
  • How does irony function spiritually?

Week 5 — The Moral Vision Clarifies

Primary Reading:
  • The Forerunner (entire book)
Supplementary Reading:
  • Beloved Prophet: Letters on art and vocation (pp. 120–150)
Discussion:
  • What values are non-negotiable here?
  • How does Gibran define freedom?
  • Where do you sense growing restraint?

Week 6 — Aphorism, Silence, and Paradox

Primary Reading:
  • Sand and Foam (entire book)
Supplementary Reading:
  • Robin Waterfield, Prophet, Chapter 9
Discussion:
  • Which aphorism felt personal?
  • Why embrace contradiction?
  • How does silence teach?

Week 7 — The Prophet Appears (Part I)

Primary Reading:
  • The Prophet: "The Coming of the Ship" through "Houses"
Supplementary Reading:
  • Robin Waterfield, Prophet, Chapter 10 (First Half)
  • CourseHero (Relevant Sections)
Discussion:
  • Why must Almustafa leave to teach?
  • Which teaching feels most alive today?
  • How does beauty replace command?

Week 8 — The Prophet Departs (Part II)

Primary Reading:
  • The Prophet: "Clothes" through "The Farewell"
Supplementary Reading:
  • Robin Waterfield, Prophet, Chapter 10 (Second Half)
  • CourseHero (Relevant Sections)
Discussion:
  • How is pain redefined?
  • What vision of death is offered?
  • Why has this book endured?

Week 9 — The Many Faces of Christ

Primary Reading:
  • Jesus, The Son of Man: Preface and first half
Supplementary Reading:
  • Robin Waterfield, Prophet, Chapter 11
Discussion:
  • Why many voices?
  • Which testimony felt most human?
  • How is holiness portrayed?

Week 10 — Vision Beyond Doctrine

Primary Reading:
  • Jesus, The Son of Man: second half
Supplementary Reading:
  • Viewing Gibran's drawings and paintings
Discussion:
  • How do image and word interact?
  • Why is embodiment central?
  • What is sacred here?

Week 11 — Return and Completion

Primary Reading:
  • The Garden of the Prophet (entire book)
Supplementary Reading:
  • Robin Waterfield, Prophet, Chapter 12
Discussion:
  • How has Almustafa changed?
  • Is wisdom quieter now?
  • What feels unfinished?

Week 12 — Legacy and Integration

Primary Reading:
  • Beloved Prophet: Letters from 1928–1931 (pp. 200–220)
  • Reread: "The Farewell" from The Prophet
Discussion:
  • Why is Gibran both loved and criticized?
  • What work or idea stays with you?
  • How might you live what you've read?

How to Access the Readings

Weekly reading averages 40–60 pages. Read slowly, reread freely, and allow space for silence and dialogue.
